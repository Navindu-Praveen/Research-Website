Literature Survey

The integration of Natural Language Processing (NLP) and Machine Learning (ML) into educational tools has revolutionized language learning, making it more personalized and effective. This transformation is particularly important in Sri Lanka, where mastering the Sinhala language is crucial for Grade 5 students preparing for the Scholarship Examination. Sinhala, with its complex grammar and morphology, poses unique challenges. Research has highlighted the importance of addressing spelling errors in educational settings, leading to the development of our Random Forest-based Spell Checker, which ensures an 82% accuracy in spelling correction. Additionally, multimodal learning methods, such as our Text-to-Speech (TTS) model with 98% pronunciation accuracy, enhance auditory learning and make the language more accessible. Despite progress in these areas, challenges remain, particularly in integrating spelling correction and TTS technologies. Our research aims to bridge these gaps by leveraging advanced NLP and ML techniques to develop a comprehensive educational toolkit. This includes not only spell checking and TTS but also Question-and-Answer generation and Named Entity Recognition (NER) systems, tailored to the linguistic nuances of Sinhala. These components ensure a holistic approach to language learning, addressing both spelling and grammatical errors, and enhancing reading comprehension and overall language proficiency. Our work sets the stage for further advancements in educational technology, improving the learning experience for Sinhala students.

In summary, the comprehensive approach to siyabasa management involves follow key components:

Random Forest-Based Spell Checker:Corrects up to 82% of spelling errors, tailored for the unique complexities of Sinhala grammar and morphology.

Custom Text-to-Speech (TTS) System: Achieves 98% pronunciation accuracy, enhancing auditory learning and accessibility for Sinhala language learners.

Neural Network-Based Question and Answer Generation: Generates relevant practice questions and answers with 82% accuracy, improving comprehension and retention.

Logistic Regression Named Entity Recognition (NER): Identifies and classifies named entities with 92% accuracy, aiding in the understanding of noun structures and improving language comprehension.

Interactive Learning Interface: Provides a user-friendly web application that integrates all tools, offering a seamless and engaging educational experience for Grade 5 students.


Research Problem

Grade 5 students in Sri Lanka face significant challenges in mastering the Sinhala language due to the absence of specialized educational tools. One of the primary issues is the lack of a customized Text-to-Speech (TTS) system tailored to the unique morphology and phonetics of Sinhala, which hampers auditory learning and pronunciation practice. Additionally, there is a notable absence of a specialized Sinhala Spell Checker, making it difficult for students to correct their spelling errors effectively, which is crucial for developing writing proficiency. Another critical gap is the lack of a dedicated Question and Answer (Q&A) Creator designed specifically for Sinhala, which limits the opportunities for interactive learning and comprehension practice. Furthermore, there is no dedicated Noun Categorization System to help students understand and classify different types of nouns within Sinhala sentences, essential for grasping the grammatical structure of the language. Addressing these research problems is vital for enhancing the learning experience and language proficiency of Grade 5 students in Sri Lanka.

Research Gap

Lack of Customized Sinhala TTS:

The current educational tools lack a Text-to-Speech (TTS) system specifically tailored for the Sinhala language, which is critical for accurate pronunciation and auditory learning. This gap limits students' ability to practice and improve their spoken Sinhala, thereby affecting overall language proficiency.

Absence of Specialized Sinhala Spell Checker:

There is no dedicated spell checker designed to address the unique spelling challenges of the Sinhala language for Grade 5 students. This absence hampers the development of accurate writing skills, as students cannot effectively correct their spelling mistakes.

No Dedicated Noun Categorization System:

The existing educational resources do not include a system to categorize nouns within Sinhala sentences, which is essential for understanding grammatical structures. Without this tool, students struggle to identify and classify different noun types, impeding their comprehension and language learning progress.

Research objectives

Develop a precise Sinhala spell checker and integrate Text-to-Speech functionality.

To develop a Sinhala Question and Answer Creation component within the web app that effectively generates questions and answers based on input sentences or paragraphs, thereby addressing the research gap in reading comprehension practice for Grade 5 students.

Develop an effective way to categorize Sinhala noun types into proper noun, common noun, collective noun, abstract noun
